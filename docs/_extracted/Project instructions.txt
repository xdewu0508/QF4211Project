DSE/QF4211 Project Instructions  
This group project is designed  to create opportunities for students to apply their 
quantitative/machine learning skills and knowledge to empirical problems related to  
cryptocurrencies. It has multiple stages, which will be carried out throughout the entire semester.  
Stage 0: Group Formation (Week 3) 
Groups will be randomly assigned. 
Stage 1: Project Proposal (Weeks 4 - 6)  
Each group is recommended to use the reference paper Cryptocurrency Trading: A Comprehensive 
Survey (Fang, Ventre, Basios et al, 2022) as a springboard to explore one of the following aspects 
of cryptocurrency trading: 
• Prediction of volatility or return 
• Crypto-asset portfolio construction 
• Market bubbles and extreme conditions 
• Technical trading strategies 
Focusing on the selected topic, each group will conduct a literature review. 
In addition to the reference paper above, each group must identify and analyse at least one other 
academic paper to develop a new project idea that builds on existing research. The project should 
aim to apply the quantitative or machine learning methods to a publicly available dataset.  
Week 5: Proposal Presentation 
All groups will submit the presentation slides by 8 February, 2026 (Sunday of Week 4). Group 
presentations will take place during Week 5’s lectures. Each presentation should be 8 - 10 minutes 
long and covers the following: 
• A summary of the literature review results 
• A brief explanation of the key reference paper’s main concepts, methodology, and results 

===== PAGE 2 =====
• A brief introduction to the group project’s research question, proposed methods, and dataset 
Week 6: Written Proposal Submission 
Incorporating the feedback from the presentation, each group must submit a written proposal by 
20 February, 2026 (Friday, Week 6), which should include:  
• A clearly defined research question and its background or motivation  
• A brief introduction of the methodology  
• Description of the dataset to be used 
• A task division plan outlining the roles and responsibilities of each group member  
• A tentative timeline for the project  
Stage 2: Project Work and Draft Submission (Weeks 7 - 11) 
Each group has till Week 11 to work collaboratively on their project.  A draft report  and 
presentation slides must be submitted by 3 April, 2026 (Friday, Week 11). 
The report should include:  
• Motivation and background of the research question  
• Literature review  
• Description of data processing  
• Explanation of methodology 
• Discussion and analysis of the results and findings  
• Limitations and conclusion  
Stage 3: Final Presentation and Peer Review (Weeks 12 - 13) 
Week 12: Project Presentation 
Each group will present their project findings and analysis during Week 12’s lecture s. The 
presentation should explain the background of and motivation for the research question, data 
processing, methodology, results and findings, as well as the limitations and conclusion.

Weeks 12 - 13: Peer Review 
Each group will also be paired with a partner group for peer review. Every member of the 
reviewing group should evaluate the partner group’s code, draft report, as well as presentation, and 
then writes a review report that includes: 
• A summary of the assigned group’s project and report  
• Verification of the code (Does it run and produce the claimed results?)  
• Constructive suggestions for improvement in terms of content, clarity, and analysis  
The peer review reports must be submitted by 15 April, 2026 (Wednesday, Week 13) and will be 
shared with the original group by 17 April, 2026 (Friday, Week 13). 
Final Report Submission Deadline 
Each group will revise their report based on the peer review feedback. The finalized report must 
be submitted by  19 April 2026  (Sunday, Reading Week ). Response and rebuttal should be 
included (as an appendix ) in the report , reflecting on the feedback received and how it was 
addressed.
